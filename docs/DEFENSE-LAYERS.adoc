== Protection Categories

=== a) Stealth (Hiding Intent)

**Goal**: Make your true browsing intent invisible to the platform's recommendation algorithms.

==== OSI Layer 7 (Application)
* **Noise Injection**: Automated clicks on off-profile content
** Platform sees: "User clicks diverse content"
** Reality: Shield is generating false signals
** Defense: Dilutes click-through rate (CTR) correlation

* **Profile Dilution**: Background tab opens
** Platform sees: Multiple topics visited simultaneously
** Reality: User only engaged with one
** Defense: Confuses session-based profiling

* **Ghost Walk Mode**: Container isolation
** Platform sees: Different user sessions
** Reality: Same user, different personas
** Defense: Prevents cross-session correlation

==== OSI Layer 6 (Presentation)
* **URL Parameter Stripping**: Removes tracking tokens
** Platform sees: Direct navigation
** Reality: Clicked from tracked link
** Defense: Breaks referrer-based attribution

==== OSI Layer 4 (Transport)
* **Timing Randomization**: Variable delays between actions
** Platform sees: Human-like interaction patterns
** Reality: Automated by extension
** Defense: Evades bot detection heuristics

---

=== b) Armouring (Blocking Attacks)

**Goal**: Prevent the platform from capturing data about you in the first place.

==== OSI Layer 7 (Application)
* **Tracker Shield**: Blocks known tracking scripts
** Blocks: Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, etc.
** Method: DOM content policy + script blocking
** Defense: Prevents behavioral data exfiltration

* **Consent Armor**: Auto-rejects non-essential cookies
** Blocks: Advertising cookies, analytics cookies
** Accepts: Only functional cookies
** Defense: GDPR/CCPA compliance enforcement

==== OSI Layer 6 (Presentation)
* **Content Security Policy (CSP) Injection**
** Blocks: Inline tracking scripts
** Allows: Only extension-approved scripts
** Defense: Prevents dynamic tracker injection

==== OSI Layer 5 (Session)
* **Storage Isolation**: Separate storage per persona
** Blocks: Cross-persona cookie sharing
** Method: Chrome container APIs
** Defense: Prevents identity correlation

==== OSI Layer 3 (Network) - Future
* **DNS-level tracker blocking** (v2.0+)
** Blocks: Tracker domains at DNS resolution
** Method: Local DNS filtering
** Defense: Prevents tracker connection entirely

---

=== c) Polymorphism (Changing Shape)

**Goal**: Present different behavioral patterns to the algorithm at different times.

==== OSI Layer 7 (Application)
* **Personas**: Switchable behavioral profiles
** Gardener persona: Slow scroll, nature content
** Tech Skeptic persona: Political news, privacy tools
** Art Student persona: Visual content, long dwell time
** Defense: Algorithm can't build stable long-term profile

* **Lens Rotation**: Switching feed transformation strategies
** Opposition Lens today → Random Walk tomorrow
** Platform sees: Inconsistent engagement patterns
** Defense: Prevents algorithm from predicting preferences

==== OSI Layer 6 (Presentation)
* **User-Agent Rotation** (v2.0+)
** Presents: Different browser/OS combinations
** Platform sees: Multiple devices
** Defense: Breaks device fingerprinting

==== OSI Layer 5 (Session)
* **Session Fragmentation**: Short sessions with varied intervals
** Pattern: 5min session, 2hr break, 15min session, 30min break
** Platform sees: Irregular engagement
** Defense: Prevents session-based behavioral modeling

---

=== d) Metamorphism (Evolving Identity)

**Goal**: Gradually shift your algorithmic identity over time to prevent lock-in.

** Week 2: 50% Gardener, 30% Art Student, 20% Tech Skeptic
** Week 3: 40% Gardener, 40% Art Student, 20% Tech Skeptic
** Defense: Gradual identity drift prevents hard classification

* **Feed Trajectory Manipulation**: Intentional bubble migration
** Current: Tech/AI cluster
** Target: Ecology/Local Politics cluster
** Method: Gradual engagement shift over weeks
** Defense: Escape filter bubble without triggering anomaly detection

==== OSI Layer 6 (Presentation)
* **Progressive Preference Masking**: Slowly hide true preferences
** Month 1: Show 80% real preferences, 20% noise
** Month 2: Show 60% real preferences, 40% noise
** Month 3: Show 40% real preferences, 60% noise
** Defense: Algorithm can't distinguish signal from noise

==== Timeline Dimension (Beyond OSI)

** Summer: Outdoor/travel content emphasis
** Winter: Indoor/creative content emphasis
** Platform sees: Natural interest evolution
** Defense: Mimics organic human behavior change

== Algorithmic Dangers & Defenses

=== Danger 1: Filter Bubble Lock-In

**Attack Vector**: Algorithm narrows content to maximize engagement, trapping user in ideological/interest echo chamber.

**Defense Layers**:
* **Layer 7**: Opposition Lens surfaces contrasting content
* **Layer 7**: Membrane Breach enables controlled escape
* **Meta**: Bubble Map shows current cluster, adjacent possibilities
* **Category**: Membrane Permeability + Metamorphism

**How It Works**:
1. Observer analyzes feed, detects homogeneity
2. User activates Opposition Lens
3. Client-side reordering prioritizes underrepresented categories
4. User clicks breach → opens 3-5 off-cluster items
5. Algorithm receives engagement signal for diverse content
6. Over time, recommendations diversify (if sustained)

**OSI Insight**: Happens entirely at Layer 7 (Application). Platform sees standard HTTP requests; can't detect client-side manipulation.

---

=== Danger 2: Behavioral Profiling

**Attack Vector**: Platform builds detailed psychological profile from engagement data (clicks, dwell time, scroll depth, session duration).

**Defense Layers**:
* **Layer 7**: Noise injection creates false positives
* **Layer 7**: Persona switching fragments behavioral data
* **Layer 5**: Storage isolation prevents cross-session correlation
* **Layer 4**: Timing randomization evades pattern detection
* **Category**: Stealth + Polymorphism

**How It Works**:
1. User activates Gardener persona
2. Shield adjusts click probability (0.3 instead of 0.7)
3. Shield injects random delays (200-800ms instead of fixed)
4. Shield opens background tabs on unrelated topics
5. Platform sees: Low confidence user profile
6. Result: Recommendations become generic, not personalized

**OSI Insight**: Multi-layer defense. Layer 7 creates false data, Layer 5 isolates identity, Layer 4 masks automation.

---

=== Danger 3: Engagement Maximization (Addiction)

**Attack Vector**: Algorithm serves infinite scroll, high-stimulation content to maximize session duration and ad exposure.

**Defense Layers**:
* **Layer 7**: Engagement Disruption rule suggests breaks
* **Layer 7**: Scroll limit enforcement
* **Layer 6**: Serendipity Lens injects low-stimulation content
* **Category**: Counter-Algorithm

**How It Works**:
1. User scrolls >50 items
2. Rule engine detects: `scroll_depth > threshold`
3. Shield displays: "You've scrolled 50 items. Take a break?"
4. If user continues: Shield injects calm/slow content
5. Serendipity Lens surfaces archival, non-trending material
6. Result: Reduced dopamine-seeking, mindful browsing

**OSI Insight**: Layer 7 rule engine monitors state, intervenes when threshold crossed. Client-side only; platform unaware.

---

=== Danger 4: Surveillance Capitalism

**Attack Vector**: Trackers follow user across web, build cross-site profile, sell to advertisers.

**Defense Layers**:
* **Layer 7**: Tracker Shield blocks scripts
* **Layer 6**: URL parameter stripping
* **Layer 5**: Storage isolation (cookies)
* **Layer 3**: DNS blocking (v2.0+)
* **Category**: Armouring

**How It Works**:
1. User visits site with Facebook Pixel
2. Shield detects: Known tracker domain
3. Shield blocks: Script load at Layer 7
4. Shield strips: `fbclid=` parameter at Layer 6
5. Shield isolates: Cookies per persona at Layer 5
6. Shield blocks: `facebook.com/tr/` at DNS Layer 3 (future)
7. Result: Tracker receives no data

**OSI Insight**: Defense-in-depth across 4 layers. Even if Layer 7 fails, Layers 6/5/3 provide fallback.

---

=== Danger 5: Algorithmic Radicalization

**Attack Vector**: Recommendation algorithm amplifies extreme content to maximize engagement (outrage, conspiracy theories).

**Defense Layers**:
* **Layer 7**: Time-Shift Lens deprioritizes trending content
* **Layer 7**: Locality Lens surfaces local/community content
* **Meta**: Bubble Map alerts user to extreme clustering
* **Category**: Membrane Permeability + Metamorphism

**How It Works**:
1. User watches political video
2. Algorithm recommends increasingly extreme content
3. Observer detects: Clustering toward extreme category
4. Bubble Map alerts: "You're drifting toward [cluster]"
5. User activates Time-Shift Lens
6. Lens deprioritizes recent/trending (where extremism lives)
7. User sees: Archival, moderate content
8. Metamorphosis scheduler: Gradual drift toward balanced clusters
9. Result: Escape radicalization pipeline

**OSI Insight**: Combines Layer 7 client-side filtering with meta-level awareness tools. User stays informed, in control.

== Why OSI Layers Matter

=== Layer 7 (Application) is Primary Battlefield

**Advantages**:
* Full control: Extension has DOM access
* Invisible to platform: Client-side mutations undetectable
* Rich context: Can analyze content semantics

**Limitations**:
* Platform can change DOM anytime (fragile)
* Can't block network requests after sent
* CPU-intensive (reordering large feeds)

**Strategy**: Do as much as possible at Layer 7, use lower layers for what Layer 7 can't handle.

---

=== Multi-Layer Defense is Redundant

If Layer 7 tracker blocking fails (script loads before extension ready):
→ Layer 6 strips tracking parameters from URLs
→ Layer 5 isolates cookies so tracker can't correlate
→ Layer 3 blocks tracker domain at DNS (future)

**Result**: Even partial failure doesn't leak data.

---

=== Meta-Layer (Beyond OSI) Enables Collective Action

OSI model is individual-focused. Algorithm Shield adds:
* **Awareness**: Bubble map, cluster visualization
* **Coordination**: Federated topology sharing (v5.0+)
* **Governance**: User-controlled rules, not black box

**This is the unique contribution**: Not just technical defense, but _informed, collective resistance_.

== Conclusion

Algorithm Shield operates primarily at **OSI Layer 7 (Application)** with strategic use of Layers 4-6 for redundancy and evasion.

**Key Insight**: The power of Algorithm Shield isn't just in blocking or hiding, but in **controlled permeability** — letting the user choose when to cross filter bubble membranes, what identity to present, and how to evolve over time.

This is **algorithmic self-defense**, not just privacy protection.

**The membrane metaphor is literal**: Like a biological membrane, Algorithm Shield is:
* **Selectively permeable**: Some things pass, some don't
* **Context-responsive**: Changes permeability based on environment
* **Self-regulating**: Maintains internal state (persona) separate from external (platform)
* **Evolvable**: Can metamorphose over time

**Bottom line**: We're not trying to beat the algorithm at its own game (that's an arms race we'd lose). We're creating a new game where the user has agency, awareness, and the ability to shape their own information diet.
